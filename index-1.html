Record 1

It's calm out. The morning sun comes through a big pane window in a long clean slab. It lays pale white across the counter and a chalkboard menu. A little tip jar gleams the same color. Dust turns slow in the light, sparse, just enough to play a bit in the air. Somewhere far off an espresso machine hisses and settles. It's warming up for the day. Someone far off, kinda cute if you look at them, wipes down a steam wand. There's something low and tinny on a radio that pulls a little at the urge to dance. But it's too quiet a place for that.

He's in the seat by the window, with the pale light casting his black hair a shade brighter as best it can. His pose is a long negotiation that ends with one leg hooked over another, sitting solid, pretending it's not expecting the next pager beep poorly, with the way it bounces with his foot tapping. His tail finds somewhere else to be. It gives a slow swish then settles, crescent at its end easy on his knee. Outside the glass the first of the festival lanterns sway on their lines, not lit yet. That's for the event later on. Moon's supposed to be waxing soon. A week or so till full. The lanterns sway. Only word spoken of the wind. A whisper. Pretty, really, paper in reds and golds strung along the length of the street. A vendor down the way is still hanging them, up on a little stepladder, taking his time. There's a girl setting out folding chairs. He turns the cup a quarter-turn. The coffee's gone down to warm.

The song on the radio ends and there's that half-second of dead air before the next one, static hanging around in the room but not talking. Staring. And in it the espresso machine ticks once, cooling, and the cute one behind the counter sets down a cup somewhere with a thak. Then the next song comes up, low and a little slower than the last. It's the kind that reminds someone of home. He looks out into the pale blue of the morning sky, head back against the glass, the cool of it on his scalp, the radio doing its slow work. The vendor moves his ladder three feet down the line and climbs back up. The girl with the folding chairs has a whole stack to go and isn't hurrying. Plenty of pale morning light. The slab of it creeps along the counter. Outside its shining on the still lanterns, hanging with no light on their ties. There's a corkboard by the door. Flyers are layered three or so deep. A guitar teacher, a band playing somewhere, last March, a missing cat. March has long since come and gone. The atmosphere. It's slow. Rolling in the way moonlight does. You don't see it before it already has its hands over every inch of ground, gripping gentle on the land, never enough to bruise. White fingers, radiant white, and too many, finding the places a body hides away. The hinge of a jaw, tight, the back of the neck under the warm of a head of hair. Pulling, grasping, holding in place so the fibers in the scalp twinge in discomfort and go taut. They feel the pulse and tilt the head back, to bare the line of the throat to another hand of pale light. Sinking deeper. The quiet command to let more take hold intensifies. Pressure in the windpipe coming in degree by degree under the inevitability of delicate fingers. There's no hurry on this pale lit morning. There's plenty of it to go. It tips the chin up, nails digging under and over in its possessive hold. To bask in the comfort. The weight of this dreary day has been held so long and it's only the morning. It would be better to put it down. And ease the discomfort. It would be better to be held in the light of the moon.

The moon lets down its ladder on the stream,
the world goes quiet as the inside of a dream,
and nothing is as heavy as it seemed.
So lay your head where all the soft things gleam.
The tide draws back. The sleeping stones are bare.
There's silver in the water, silver in the air,
and something kind and patient waiting there
to take the weight you weren't built to bear.
The lanterns dim. The current finds your feet.
It pulls so slow it almost feels like sleep,
like being warm again, and held, and sweet,
in arms that ask for no promise you can't keep.
So set it down. There's nowhere left to go.
The light comes gentle and it comes in low.
Lay back. Let the long water take you slow.
There's rest beneath the moon. You'll come to know.
And if you wake, you'll wake to find it true.
That all that pretty light was made just for you.

So —

BREEP. BREEP. BREEP-BZZT.

The pager. Cheap and electric and ugly. It's super thick from all the electromagnetic shielding they had to put in it. His hand comes up slow to his throat, fingers spreading careful over it. The pressure is gone but the ache is still there. He swallows. The pager keeps beeping, the amber light on it yelling for attention. But his eyes climb to the window, up past the wind-waving lanterns. Up in the sky is the morning sun, and a cloud that rolls along too slow. And barely visible is a blue-washed outline of a white gibbous. Lingering, as the pager still beeps.

Designation Notes

Specimen is a Moonlin — a humanoid selenic entity that ordinarily operates under the direct command of the lunar intelligence catalogued as SA-001 redacted. Moonlin function as extensions of her will and do not, under normal conditions, exhibit independent cognition.

EA-009 is an apostate, catalogued selenic anomaly SA-7A. The subject retains full selenic attunement and continues to receive the directive of SA-001, but currently appears to operate under its own autonomy. Observation over time has led to the determination that this is not a malfunction but a sustained act of will. The subject was reclassified from contained anomaly to operating asset (EA-009) on this basis.

EA-009 operates as an investigator and first responder for events and subjects deemed under the authority of the Selenic Containment Branch. Agent 009 is to be monitored closely and cautiously in accordance with guidelines established in this document and all supporting documents related to anomalous entity SA-001 redacted. Agent 009 is fit for service, with a risk assessment and deployment authorization necessary for any operations conducted within 48 hours before and after the full phase of the moon. During this window the agent's attunement to SA-001 intensifies, and his continued autonomy cannot be guaranteed. EA-009 has demonstrated reliable conduct and is cleared for standard deployment under escort, or monitored deployment when operating unaccompanied.

Description

Physical specifications are variable due to the subject's minor shapeshifting abilities, but generally take on consistent characteristics in what is deemed its two "default forms"

  • Height: approx. 7'4" (Moonlin) / 5'11" (Human)
  • Apparent Age: mid twenties (Human)
  • Dermis: pale, cool-toned, faintly luminescent
  • Cranial: elongated pointed aural structures
  • Ocular: two opposed luminous crescents; no visible sclera or pupil. Emission intensifies with correlation under study
  • Oral: normally a solid, seamless feature; lower face capable of splitting to reveal full dentition. Fangs prominent.
  • Mane: dark, dense, semi-mobile in absence of air current. Cause undetermined.
  • Posterior: semi-reptilian caudal structure terminating in a crescent formation.

Containment Procedures

EA-009 is held in Residential Containment Unit 4 (climate-neutral). Subject is cooperative and ambulatory under escort. Standard restraints unnecessary; subject has not attempted egress.

  • Direct flash photography prohibited.
  • Personnel are not to discuss SA-001 in the subject's presence outside sanctioned interview.
  • During full lunar phase, attunement spikes; observation staffing is doubled. Subject is not to be deployed within 48 hours before or after full phase without risk assessment and deployment authorization.
  • Personnel reporting "a feeling of being watched from above" near the unit are to report to medical and rotate off-duty.
  • Subject has developed a marked preference for coffee and is permitted regular provision. Disposition improves noticeably when supply is maintained. Staff have, without prompting, begun bringing it unbidden.

Capabilities

  • Electromagnetic interference: Disrupts nearby electronic and electromagnetic systems, passively and at will. Lighting, instrumentation, and recording equipment affected in proximity.
  • Radio communication: Projects and receives through radio-band transmission, manifesting as voice over active equipment. Has spoken through facility comms without authorization on redacted occasions.
  • Short-range translocation: Displaces itself instantaneously across short distances. Range limited; no sustained transit observed. Silent; leaves brief electromagnetic disturbance at origin and arrival.
  • Selenic emission — "moonbeam": Discharges a directed beam of luminous energy. Offensive potential confirmed; full output untested under controlled conditions.

Because the subject remains fully attuned to the selenic command network while refusing its directive, EA-009 can detect, locate, and identify other Moonlin and a range of adjacent anomalous entities prior to conventional instrument confirmation. The specimen is, functionally, a living tracking device, and is deployed under escort for tracking and identification operations.

Containment Note: The translocation signature is the only reliable warning of subject movement; EM sensors at the unit are maintained for this purpose. Recorded observation cannot be considered secure — interference and unauthorized comms access mean no proximate log is presumed tamper-free. Portions of this file may have been read by its subject.

Risk Assessment

  • Physical threat (unprovoked): Moderate
  • Egress risk: Low
  • Electromagnetic / comms compromise: Moderate
  • Anomalous capability deployment: High
  • Intelligence compromise: High
  • Reclamation by SA-001: Unresolved

The subject is a threat, but the threat is principally one of uncertainty. Two questions remain unresolved and may not be resolvable by observation alone.

First — whether the apostasy is genuine. A subject that refused SA-001 and one that merely performs refusal, in order to embed within the facility and act on her behalf, would be, to all available instrumentation, indistinguishable.

Second — whether genuine refusal is permanent. The claim of SA-001 on a Moonlin is not understood to expire. No basis exists to estimate how long the subject can hold a door shut against the thing on the other side of it, or whether that decision remains the subject's to make.

Continued utility is judged to outweigh the risk. This judgment is reviewed quarterly and has not changed.

SA-001 — redacted

SA-001 is the first catalogued selenic anomaly and the origin of the classification. Authority designation redacted. All Moonlin, including EA-009, are bound to it from creation.

  • Designation: redacted (SA-001)
  • Linkage: Lumen Noumenal
  • Documented Effect: Lunar Languor
  • Observability: Noumenal; inferred only by effect

The linkage between SA-001 and the bound, designated Lumen Noumenal, cannot be detected, located, or severed by any available instrument. Its existence is inferred solely from subject behavior. SA-001 itself has never been directly observed; the Authority records only its effects.

Chief among these is Lunar Languor: a documented state of heavy, euphoric compliance reported by linked subjects and, in lesser degree, by personnel in proximity to active linkage. Subjects describe it not as compulsion but as solace, relief, or other generally positive affectations. The reports of subjects, or of individuals suspected to be under the effects of SA-001's influence, are not under any circumstances to be considered accurate. EA-009 refers to SA-001 as "Lady Moonlight" and has not elaborated on record.

  • redacted — ██ personnel. No signs of struggle, no external entry. redacted found redacted, arranged.
  • redacted walked redacted during full phase and did not redacted. Remains redacted. Smiling.

The van rolls easy down the access road, no rush, heat shimmering off the asphalt and the AC losing a quiet war against the noon sun. Julie has one wrist draped over the wheel, ponytail spilling down the seatback, the other hand fishing a sour candy out of a bag in the cupholder without looking. "If this gun thing actually works, mmm," she says around it, "I'm chilling tonight and nobody's allowed to call me. That's the deal, alright."

"Yeah, like they'd listen if we asked," Denny says from the back, headset crooked on one ear. He has a photo wedged into the console light, his fiancée mid-laugh, and he keeps glancing at it between readings. "Claire says good luck, by the way. God, she's perfect."

"You've said that four times since the gate," Cass murmurs from the passenger seat, not opening his eyes. He has the gun across his lap, a ring of dark housing with a hollow circle in the center, one clawed finger tapping slow on the rim. "I'm starting to think you're trying to convince yourself."

"I'm not. She's—" Denny stops. "Okay, that's fair, but she is."

Cass huffs something close to a laugh and finally cracks one eye, the pale of it gleaming into the dim cabin shade. "Relax. She sounds great. Anybody who'd marry you has gotta be a good person." He rolls his neck. In the background, "Hey, what does that mean..." Then Cass again, "Six miles. Then I get to find out if this thing's just a fancy flashlight."

"Telemetry's green, skies are clear, site's just past the—" Denny's screen blooms red. The cabin lights with it, and a flat ACA tone climbs over the road noise, that specific pitch they all know. BREACH EVENT stamps itself across his display. "...Huh. Or. The test came to us." Exhausted and a bit gritty, like she has gummies in her mouth, Julie squints up through the windshield. "...That's a rogue tranq drone. One of ours. Great. Love when our own stuff goes feral." A beat, then drier still, "I'm betting flashlight."

Julie hasn't even stopped before it breaks skyward. A drone, ACA plating gone matte, a fat dart-rig slung under its belly. Denny goes pale at his screen. "That's a heavy-suppression unit. Cass — those things are rated to tranq down A-TA-73. Seventy-three. If it tags you you're not waking up for a week."

"Cool. Love that." The shadow in the sky banks low and lines up on the van. "Okay! We're doing this!" Julie floors it onto open road, suspension shrieking.

"Get me close, Julie!" The seat goes empty. A blink, a cold pull of air, and Cass is on the roof, boots planted, wind tearing at him. The drone wheels and its underside splits open, a dart-rack rotating up.

The first volley skitters off the steel by his boots. He drops flat as Julie swerves. He poofs back into the cabin next to her. "Warn me when you do that!" — "Get back up there, idiot! It's shooting at us!" He deadpans and turns to black mist — the drone jukes, Cass appearing back on the hull of the van. The next dart punches deep into his shoulder and sticks, pumping through the skin. He tears it out, gritting, pale light beading at the hole. His hand comes up to his headset, one eye wincing. "Denny, it hit me. How much time I got left!?" he grits, already feeling it, cold sludge down his arm, the moon-pull in his chest stuttering. Denny's voice cracks over the wind: "That's a 4000-pound dose in a buck-eighty body, that's — that's really bad, Moonman! Get it done now!"

The other arm has already gone dead, hanging limp at his side. Cass sways on the hull, the horizon tilting like the whole sky has come loose. More darts ping off the steel around him, and he barely registers them. He brings the gun up anyway, the prism trunk of it angled toward the dull plates of the drone.

The aperture lights, the nested rings begin to turn, two, then three, counter-rotating, dragging the noon glare into themselves and bleeding it back out cold. Pale light pools in the hollow center, the pull of it wrenching him so hard the tranq fog isn't so bad. "...There it is," he breathes.

The moonbeam tears loose — a ribbon, a flat wafer of silver fire wide as his hand and thin as a blade, searing a line across the bright daytime sky, the air along its edge shivering like heat haze run backwards. It crosses the distance instantly. Where it meets the drone, it shears, dragging through matte plating like the metal had only ever been a suggestion. The dart-rig peels away in a screaming arc. Stabilizers spin off glinting. The beam carves the frame near in half and the two pieces simply part, light pouring out of the seam, before the scrap drops and craters a hundred yards out in a long tumbling scrape of smoke and severed glow.

The recoil and the dose hit together. The gun's rings spin down, the silver guttering out, and Cass folds. He appears face down on the floor in the van cabin, groaning. Both Julie and Denny huddle to him in an excited hurry. "Moonman!" They give each other an evil smile. "We get to go home today!" Then Julie, "Moonman? You alright, dude? Moonman..."

Filing Note

The official designation is AMOEBA, assigned at intake for the subject's gelatinous, form-unstable morphology. Field and observation staff have without exception adopted the spelling "AMEEBA," which now appears across nearly all working notes and recovered transcripts. A directive reminding personnel of the correct spelling was issued and is, per the submitting clerk, the only document in the active file still using it.

Designation Notes

Subject is an unclassified anomalous entity, first logged after a series of property-disturbance and unexplained-consumption reports in the river district. No taxonomy has been established; the entity is a shapeshifter and does not hold a stable form long enough for conventional classification to complete. It exhibits no observed hostility. Threat to the city is assessed on property damage, consumption, and the difficulty of tracking. Recovery has not been achieved. The entity does not appear to be evading the Authority so much as it's unconcerned with it.

Description

Physical specifications are unreliable. The subject is a shapeshifter and adopts a wide range of forms. Its default — the form it holds when unobserved or at rest — is a small grey, semi-translucent cat. Observations below describe tendencies across sightings.

  • Mass: variable; increases following feeding events.
  • Composition: semi-translucent, gelatinous. Holds shape under its own cohesion; "pours" and re-forms at will.
  • Luminescence: faint emission at extremities under low light. Cause undetermined. Present across all forms.
  • Acoustic signature: a low, motorless hum when content. Field staff compare it to "a refrigerator running in another room."
  • Affect: reads as affectionate. Approaches warm surfaces and, on two logged occasions, personnel. Has harmed neither.

Containment Procedures

No containment unit currently holds the subject. The following are recovery-and-handling directives for field encounters.

  • Do not corner the subject. Cornered, it changes form and relocates; it does not fight.
  • Subject is to be recovered, not neutralized. Standing order: the entity has caused no injury and is not to be treated as hostile absent new evidence.
  • Consumption hazard: subject feeds opportunistically and indiscriminately. Secure loose materials, foodstuffs, and small unattended equipment in its vicinity. Two field recorders are unaccounted for and presumed consumed.
  • Subject is attracted to warmth. Heated surfaces, idling vehicle hoods, and active equipment draw it. This is the most reliable means of predicting its position.

Capabilities

  • Shapeshifting: full-body morphological change, rapid and effortless. No upper limit on form variation has been established. This is the primary obstacle to classification.
  • Consumption: voracious and broad. Ingests organic and inorganic material alike and gains mass from doing so. No upper bound on appetite observed. The behavior reads as feeding, not aggression.
  • Translocation by form: crosses ground by reshaping — flattening, pouring, re-forming — rather than by teleportation. Difficult to follow; not instantaneous.
  • Cohesion: maintains integrity across radical shape change and can re-form after being dispersed. No method of permanent dispersal is known. The Authority has not attempted one.

Risk Assessment

  • Physical threat (unprovoked): Negligible
  • Egress / evasion risk: High (no fixed form; cannot be reliably held or tracked)
  • Property / consumption hazard: Moderate
  • Civilian exposure risk: Moderate (visibility, not violence)
  • Classification confidence: Low

The subject is a problem the Authority does not have a shape for. It is not dangerous in any manner the threat framework was built to measure — it has hurt no one, threatened no one, and fled nothing. It eats, it changes, it seeks warmth, and it cannot be caught. The file remains open because the Authority does not know what the subject is, and the absence of a hostile act is not, by current procedure, the same as the absence of a threat.

One reviewer has appended a single line to the assessment, unsanctioned, and it has not been struck: "We are not chasing this thing because it is dangerous. We are chasing it because we cannot name it, and that is our failing, not its."
